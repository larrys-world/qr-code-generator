What is a QR Code?

QR (Quick Response) codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can store various types of data. Originally developed in 1994 for the automotive industry in Japan, QR codes have become ubiquitous in our digital world, enabling quick access to websites, contact information, WiFi credentials, and much more.

Popular QR Code Uses

  • Website URLs: Direct users to your website or landing page
  • WiFi Access: Share network credentials without typing passwords
  • Contact Cards: Share vCard information instantly
  • Payment Links: Enable quick mobile payments
  • Restaurant Menus: Contactless menu viewing
  • Event Tickets: Digital ticketing and check-ins
  • Product Information: Link to manuals, reviews, or specifications
  • Social Media: Quick follows and profile sharing

Error Correction Levels Explained

QR codes have built-in error correction that allows them to be scanned even when partially damaged:

  • Low (L) - 7%: Best for clean environments and maximum data capacity
  • Medium (M) - 15%: Default level, good balance of size and reliability
  • Quartile (Q) - 25%: Higher reliability, good for outdoor use
  • High (H) - 30%: Maximum reliability, ideal for industrial environments

QR Code Best Practices

  • Test your QR code with multiple devices before printing
  • Ensure sufficient contrast between foreground and background colors
  • Leave adequate white space (quiet zone) around the QR code
  • Use vector format (SVG) for printed materials to ensure quality
  • Consider the scanning distance when choosing size
  • Use URL shorteners for long web addresses to create simpler QR codes

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the QR codes free to use?

Yes, all QR codes generated here are completely free for personal and commercial use.

Do QR codes expire?

No, QR codes don't expire. They will work as long as the content they link to remains available.

What's the difference between PNG and SVG?

PNG is a raster format best for digital use, while SVG is a vector format that scales perfectly for print.

Can I track QR code scans?

The QR code itself doesn't track scans, but you can use URL shorteners with analytics for tracking.
